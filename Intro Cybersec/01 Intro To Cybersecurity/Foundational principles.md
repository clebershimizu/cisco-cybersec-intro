The foundational principles for protecting information

1 - Confidentiality
2 - Integrity
3 - Availability

Confidentiality: set of rules that prevents sensitive information from being disclesed do unauthorized people, resources and processes. Methods to ensure confidentiality include data encryption, identity proofing and two factor authentication.

Integrity: ensures that system information or processes are protected from intentional or accidental modification. One way to ensure integrity in to use a hash function or checksum.

Availability: means that authorized users are able to access systems and data when and where needed and those that do not meet established conditions, are not. This can be achieved by maintaining equipment, preforming hardware repairs, keeping operating systems and software up to date and creating backups.
